BXL Street Art

Countless Belgian and international artists have taken over Brussels and decorated it with colourful graffiti artworks.

Brussels is basically an open-air street art gallery. No matter where you turn, the city is decorated with subtle, as well as enormous wall paintings, often with a mischievous sprinkle of Belgian humour.

From comic book characters to tags, masterpieces and art installations, the history of Brussels street art tracks subculture movements and illustrates Brussels paradoxical, relation to graffiti and street art.

While municipalities support graffiti pieces that fall in line with their cultural views and commission artists to create big frescos in controlled environments, they erase and try to prevent other types of street art.

Fais Le Trottoir ASBL is an organization that fosters public awareness of the different forms of street art in Brussels.

For someone wanting to get a quick glimpse of its non-commissioned graffiti, I recommend taking one of their tours, walking along the canal, visiting Brussels-Chapel train station or wondering around in Saint-Gilles and Ixelles.
